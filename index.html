About

I develop autonomous systems for satellite tracking and space situational awareness, focusing on real-time orbital mechanics computation and threat assessment algorithms. My work combines advanced astronomical calculations with modern cloud-native architectures to create scalable, precise tracking solutions.

Currently building comprehensive satellite tracking platforms that handle 20,000+ space objects with sub-kilometer accuracy, featuring autonomous data acquisition, conjunction threat analysis, and real-time 3D visualization capabilities.

The goal is to contribute to space safety through improved tracking accuracy, automated threat detection, and accessible visualization tools that can inform future space traffic management systems.

My Journey

I graduated with an Electrical and Electronic Engineering degree from Bangladesh, but honestly, my real journey started when I stumbled into NASA's online events. I wasn't planning to fall in love with space technology, but there I was, actively participating in Mars missions, moon observation, NASA DART, NASA EPDC, and NASA GLOBE citizen science projects from my room in Bangladesh.

Here's where things got interesting. While volunteering for NASA GLOBE, I first learned about the real-time use of satellites Aqua and Terra. That's when it hit me - I always wanted to work for any space agency. I even had this eager dream of attending seminars or webinars in person. But reality check: I later realized you can't work or intern at NASA unless you're a US citizen. Well, that was disappointing.

So there I was, stuck in Bangladesh with limited resources and almost zero opportunities to do anything different outside of hardcore academia. What do you do? I focused on self-learning with whatever public resources I could find. During this phase, I discovered something about myself - I learn way better by listening rather than just reading. Based on this understanding, I started teaching on an experimental basis to know myself better.

Interestingly, I discovered that I can explain technical stuff in much simpler ways with examples from what's actually happening technically. I go very deep with different entangled thoughts that often come out as better ideas. Then I work on those ideas, explain them again, and come up with even better concepts. I probably could do better in cognitive science related questions because when I question things, it follows certain patterns by default.

Then I started applying to different universities in many countries - France, Italy, UK, India. Successfully got offers from many with partial scholarships or fee waivers. In 2024, I chose France and got my visa. But here's where the complex story begins. Due to foreign currency scarcity in Bangladesh and bank officers giving me misinformation, I faced several challenges paying for accommodation. Even after purchasing air tickets and foreign currency exchange, 7 days before departure, I had to cancel everything.

Now many people will be saying with raised eyebrows, "What? How many succeeded studying abroad while you failed even after getting the visa?" I want to give you just a small hint - there was a phase in Bangladesh where even creating a student account for studying abroad was difficult. And regarding "many" - so many students process their applications via 3rd party brokers. From applying to the university to writing your SOP, they do everything on behalf of you.

Here I am, allergic to any broker who does that. This is unfair. My simple logic is: if you can't write your Statement of Purpose or Statement of Intent, then in simple words, you don't know yourself yet. If you don't know what you want in life, what your goals are, then sorry to say, how do you actually continue your study? Because you have to do thesis or internship in your last semester or last year of university.

The story is very complex, but I want to say that I did predictions based on my entangled thoughts that this problem might arise in my career because I was noticing how some banking officials were giving misinformation. I calculated every step, and yes, not only that - I've noticed many such things even before happening. That's why sometimes I say I could have done better in cognitive science related fields.

Okay, enough about others. I am still actively working on myself, learning and trying to implement. Currently enrolled (September 2025) in MSc program in Communications Engineering at PoliTO as an international student, waiting for visa approval. And regarding my current project, it was sparkling since I knew about those two satellites Aqua and Terra. Now it's time for building cool stuff.

No one can ignore technology, even if they want to. That's why my philosophy is to create an autonomous utopian future. However, the current hype is AI, but if you think AI means only chatbots or writing tools, then you are again mistaken. There's so much more to explore and build.

Technical Background

What I'm Actually Building

Space situational awareness systems that don't just track satellites but predict where things might go wrong. I'm talking autonomous satellite tracking, orbital mechanics modeling that actually works in real-time, and threat assessment algorithms that can tell you if two objects might have a bad day in space. Plus real-time 3D visualization because why just have data when you can see it happening?

My Technical Arsenal

Languages I Think In: TypeScript, Python, Rust, Go
Frameworks I Live With: React, Node.js, Flask, FastAPI
Orbital Mechanics Tools: Skyfield, SGP4, Astropy, GMAT
Making Things Visual: CesiumJS, Three.js, WebGL
Infrastructure That Actually Works: Docker, Kubernetes, MongoDB, Redis

But honestly, tools are just tools. What matters is understanding how satellites actually move, how to predict their paths, and how to build systems that don't break when you're tracking 20,000+ objects simultaneously.

Current Research & Development

I'm developing next-generation autonomous satellite tracking systems with sub-kilometer accuracy. Not because it sounds cool, but because current systems often miss critical details. I'm also investigating machine learning approaches for conjunction prediction - basically teaching computers to predict when satellites might crash into each other before it happens.

Plus, I'm creating open-source tools for space situational awareness because this stuff shouldn't be locked behind corporate walls. Space affects everyone, so the tools to understand it should be accessible to everyone.

Why This Actually Matters

I'm building accessible tools for space safety research, contributing to open-source astronomical software, and advancing automated threat detection for space traffic management. Think of it as building the air traffic control system for space - except space is much bigger, objects move much faster, and there's no room for error.

The goal isn't just to track things, but to make space safer for future missions, whether they're scientific research, commercial ventures, or human exploration. Because honestly, we're going to need clean orbital highways if we want to keep exploring.
