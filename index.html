About

I develop autonomous systems for satellite tracking and space situational awareness, focusing on real-time orbital mechanics computation and threat assessment algorithms. My work combines advanced astronomical calculations with modern cloud-native architectures to create scalable, precise tracking solutions.

Currently building comprehensive satellite tracking platforms that handle 20,000+ space objects with sub-kilometer accuracy, featuring autonomous data acquisition, conjunction threat analysis, and real-time 3D visualization capabilities.

The goal is to contribute to space safety through improved tracking accuracy, automated threat detection, and accessible visualization tools that can inform future space traffic management systems.

My Journey

I graduated with an Electrical and Electronic Engineering degree from Bangladesh, but honestly, my real journey started when I stumbled into NASA's online events. I wasn't planning to fall in love with space technology, but there I was, actively participating in Mars missions, moon observation, and NASA GLOBE citizen science projects from my room. While volunteering for NASA GLOBE, I first learned about the real-time use of satellites Aqua and Terra. That's when it hit me, I always wanted to work for a space agency.

So there I was, stuck in Bangladesh with limited resources. I focused on self-learning with whatever public resources I could find. During this phase, I discovered I learn way better by listening than just reading. Based on this, I started teaching experimentally to know myself better. Interestingly, I discovered that I can explain technical stuff in much simpler ways. I go very deep with different entangled thoughts that often come out as better ideas.

I am still actively working on myself, learning and trying to implement. Currently enrolled (September 2025) in an MSc program in Communications Engineering at PoliTO as an international student, waiting for visa approval. And regarding my current project, it was sparkling since I knew about those two satellites Aqua and Terra. Now it's time for building cool stuff.

No one can ignore technology, even if they want to. That's why my philosophy is to create an autonomous utopian future. The current hype is AI, but if you think AI means only chatbots or writing tools, then you are mistaken. There's so much more to explore and build.

Technical Background

Space situational awareness systems that don't just track satellites but predict where things might go wrong. I'm talking autonomous satellite tracking, real-time orbital mechanics modeling, and threat assessment algorithms. Plus real-time 3D visualization because why just have data when you can see it?

Frameworks: React, Node.js, Flask, FastAPI
Orbital Mechanics: Skyfield, SGP4, Astropy, GMAT
Visualization: CesiumJS, Three.js, WebGL
Infrastructure: Docker, Kubernetes, MongoDB, Redis

Why This Matters

I'm building accessible tools for space safety research and advancing automated threat detection for space traffic management. Think of it as building the air traffic control system for space, except space is much bigger, objects move much faster, and there's no room for error.
